## 2026-04-30 - Spec 5: Counterfactual Replay / Fork (v0.8.12)

**Action:** Implemented Spec 5 of the AI-first dev roadmap: counterfactual replay primitive. `SessionReplayer.forkAt(targetTick)` opens a paused world at a chosen tick, returns a chainable `ForkBuilder` with `.replace(originalSequence, newCommand)` / `.insert(newCommand)` / `.drop(originalSequence)` / `.snapshot()` / `.run({ untilTick, sink?, sourceLabel? })`. `run()` materializes a fresh `SessionBundle` whose timeline diverges from source per the substitutions, plus a structured `Divergence` summary (per-tick split counts, `firstDivergentTick`, `commandSequenceMap`, `equivalent` flag). Standalone `diffBundles(a, b, options?)` walks the union of tick ranges and produces per-tick command/event/state deltas plus separate metadata/markers/attachments deltas (excluded from `equivalent` per ADR 7).

**Architecture:**

- 7 ADRs in DESIGN.md v4: forks produce normal `SessionBundle`s (not subtype); single-tick substitution chained via re-fork; inline divergence covers commands+events only (state diffs deferred to `diffBundles`); substituted commands go through `world.submitWithResult()` + sequence post-mapping (cannot inherit source sequences because `WorldSnapshotV5` doesn't carry `nextCommandResultSequence`); fork's recorder defaults to `MemorySink({ allowSidecar: true })`; no fork-of-fork tracking; `diffBundles` is symmetric without `commandSequenceMap` and asymmetric with it.
- `applyTickDiff(snapshot, diff): WorldSnapshot` is a new internal helper (NOT exported from `src/index.ts`) folding TickDiffs over all six dimensions. Produces partial-hydration snapshots (rng/componentOptions/config don't appear in TickDiff). Safe inside `diffSnapshots` consumers (which exclude rng by design); external consumers wanting "snapshot at tick N" should use `replayer.openAt(N).serialize()` (= `replayer.stateAtTick(N)`).
- Step 6 IMPL uses a two-walk approach: command-stream walk (groups by submissionTick, catches divergence at pre-advance failure ticks where SessionTickEntry was never written — commands are recorded BEFORE `world.step()`) + SessionTickEntry walk (events only, no double-count).
- Step 7 equivalence test (the load-bearing invariant): a no-substitution fork from any midTick produces a `ForkResult` where `divergence.equivalent === true` AND the fork's bundle is structurally equivalent to source's slice over `[midTick, persistedEndTick]` modulo per-recorder noise (sessionId, recordedAt, sequence range, metrics, etc.). Verified across multiple bundle sizes + edge ticks (startTick, near-endTick).
